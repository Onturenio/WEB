Short CV

I studied Physics at the University of Murcia, Spain. I graduated in 2005, receiving the Extraordinary B.Sc. Award for the best student in the degree of Physics. In 2006 I got a scholarship for my Master studies in Quantitative Finances in Madrid. In 2007 I got a PhD fellowship (funded by the Spanish Ministry of Education) to course my PhD studies. In December 2012 I got a PhD in Science (European mention). From October 2011 to April 2012 I was funded by the SPEQTRES project. Since May 2011 I am working at Helmholtz-Zentrum Geesthacht funded by the PRIME2 project. My main topic of research covers paleoclimate science using Regional Climate Models to downscale General Circulation Model simulations. I have been guest researcher in several international institutions, such as the Environmental Sciences Research Centre in Canada, the Justus-Liebig University Giessen in Germany or the European Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecast in the United Kingdom. I have contributed to a number of international conferences in Europe and the United States (EGU general assembly and AGU fall meeting, among others). I have published several papers in JCR-included peer-reviewed journals. Currently, I'm involved in several scientific projects such as SPEQTRES, PRIME2, SALVÁ-SINOBAS, ESCENA or EOLMAP.
